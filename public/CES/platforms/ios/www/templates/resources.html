Group Residential Housing

Group Residential Housing (GRH) is a State-funded income-supplement for qualified participants that can help pay for housing and housing supports. GRH pays for rent, utilities, household needs and, under some circumstances, food and/or services for people, who have low income, are over 18 years old and have a disability or are over 65 years old.

Before the funds may be used, the participant, the living situation, & provider must meet statutory eligibility requirements. Eligibility of providers for GRH funding is determined by St Louis County. Participant eligibility is determined by St Louis County Economic Assistance and is based on income, assets and disability.

Subsidized Housing

Subsidized housing provides rental assistance to qualified very-low to low income individuals and families. Subsidies work on the premise that renters should not pay more than 30% of their gross income for housing. Assistance can take several forms including individual housing vouchers, project-based assistance, public housing and tenant-based assistance just to name a few. Subsidy programs are usually administered by Housing and Redevelopment Authorities (HRA’s) or Public Housing Authorities (PHA’s).
